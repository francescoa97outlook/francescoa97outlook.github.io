Research & Work Experience

Oct 2021 – Jun 2025
INAF–OATo · Turin, Italy

Software & Scientific Analyst: Exoplanet Atmospheres

INAF (National Institute of Astrophysics)

Two phases at the INAF–OATo exoplanet group: a close collaboration (Oct 2021 – Apr 2024), followed by a research fellowship (May 2024 – Jun 2025) on the graphical interface and the optimization of a reduction-and-analysis pipeline for high-resolution spectra, under the ESPLORA project (Exoplanet Spectroscopy at high resolution to Probe their Lost Origins by Revealing their Atmospheric compositions; PRIN 2022).

  • Lead developer of GUIBRUSHR®, the tool I use to measure exoplanet atmospheric compositions from high- and low-resolution spectra: rewrote the original IDL codebase in Python and added CPU and GPU parallelization of the radiative-transfer step, with differential-evolution MCMC retrievals built on petitRADTRANS & PyratBay.
  • Built the core modules (Data Reduction, Cross-Correlation, and Telluric Removal with adaptive PCA) and the database backend, migrated from PostgreSQL to SQLite3.
  • Enabled simultaneous analysis of low-resolution data (James Webb Space Telescope, Hubble Space Telescope) and high-resolution spectra (GIANO-B at the Telescopio Nazionale Galileo, IGRINS, and the future ANDES at the Extremely Large Telescope) within a single workflow.
  • Developed pyExoRaMa, a Python tool for the radius–mass diagram of exoplanets.
May 2020 – Jan 2024
INAF–OATo · Turin, Italy

Software & Scientific Analyst: Solar Physics

INAF (National Institute of Astrophysics)

A sequence of INAF fellowships and collaborations (2020–2024) spanning solar-coronagraph instrumentation and space-weather forecasting.

  • PROBA-3 / ASPIICS: end-to-end MATLAB simulator for the Shadow Position Sensor (SPS) and the OPSE occulter sensors, later managing the in-flight SPS software.
